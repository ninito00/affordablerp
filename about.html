Tenant-Centered News and Housing Resources

Mission Statement

RPAL envisions a Rogers Park where all people can afford to live and contribute to a neighborhood that belongs to them. We recognize that Rogers Park is not unique in its need for community defense of these rights. Here, as elsewhere, this begins with the fight against profit-driven development and displacement of tenants, and with building a network of informed tenants committed to community care and collective power.

List of Intentions

  • Identify buildings which are under threat of predatory redevelopment
  • Identify property owners/LLCs/landlords whose properties are in violation of tenants rights
  • Create a mailbox where tenants can voice their complaints as a way to connect and/or amplify concerns
  • Distribute information about tenants rights, resources, development projects, and history of RP / anti gentrification work
  • Build relationships with RP community organizations and other tenants organizations in Chicago and elsewhere
  • Connect our work with larger, connected issues like deportation and migrants rights, workers rights, environmental justice, and the project of Settler Colonialism
